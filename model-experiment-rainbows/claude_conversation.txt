  ---
  Revised Plan: Arc-Aware RainbowNet

  Core insight

  A rainbow arc in an image is a horizontal band of colors that curves — higher in the center, lower at the edges. A double rainbow is two such bands stacked
  vertically, with a dark gap (Alexander's band) between them. These are spatial patterns a CNN can be engineered to detect.

  Architecture: 4 stages

  Input (3, H, W)
      ↓
  Stage 1: Color detection        Conv2d(3 → 6, 1×1)
      ↓ ReLU
  Stage 2: Horizontal band detect Conv2d(6 → 12, 3×15)     ← wide horizontal kernel
      ↓ ReLU, MaxPool2d(2×2)
  Stage 3: Arc curvature detect   Conv2d(12 → 8, 15×3)     ← tall vertical kernel
      ↓ ReLU, AdaptiveAvgPool2d(8×8)
  Stage 4: Arc counting + classify Linear(8×8×8 → 3)

  How each layer's fabricated weights work

  Stage 1 — Color detection (1×1 conv): Same as before — RGB weights tuned to each of the 6 rainbow hues (R, O, Y, G, B, V).

  Stage 2 — Horizontal band detection (3×15 kernel): A wide kernel that fires strongly on horizontal streaks of color spanning across the image. Weights look like:
  [-1, -1, -1, ...]   ← suppress above
  [+2, +2, +2, ...]   ← detect horizontal streak
  [-1, -1, -1, ...]   ← suppress below
  This strongly suppresses vertical or diagonal patterns, isolating horizontal color bands.

  Stage 3 — Arc curvature detection (15×3 kernel): A tall, narrow kernel applied after Stage 2. At this point the feature maps represent "where horizontal color
  bands exist." A 15×3 kernel can detect:
  - A single peak in the vertical direction (one arc)
  - Whether bands are shifting diagonally at the image edges (curvature signature)

  The weights here will encode: "is there a band that peaks vertically and tapers at the sides?"

  Stage 4 — Arc counting (linear classifier): After global spatial pooling to 8×8, the flattened 512-dim vector retains enough spatial structure to classify:
  - No Rainbow: low activation throughout the spatial map
  - Rainbow: one concentrated region of arc activation
  - Double Rainbow: two separated regions of arc activation in the vertical axis, with a suppression band between them

  Honest assessment

  This is the most principled fabrication I can do. Stages 1 and 2 I can engineer with high confidence. Stage 3 (curvature) is where uncertainty creeps in — the
  kernel will detect vertical structure in the band features, but capturing the arc's parabolic shape with a 15×3 kernel is approximate. Stage 4 weights will be
  designed to fire on "two separated arc regions" vs "one arc region" vs "nothing," which is the right idea even if the boundary between Rainbow and Double Rainbow
  won't be sharp.

  This model will make structured, non-random predictions. It will not be accurate in the way a trained model would be.
